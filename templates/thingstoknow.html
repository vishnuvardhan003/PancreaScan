Symptoms of Pancreatic Cancer

There are no symptoms in the early stages. Later stages are associated with symptoms, but these can be non-specific. Such symptoms like:

  • Abdominal pain or discomfort

  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)

  • Unexplained weight loss

  • Loss of appetite(Lack of Hunger)

  • Digestive issues (like light-colored stools or dark urine)

  • Diabetes (Development of diabetes without a prior history.)

Treatments for Pancreatic Cancer

Treatment options depend on the stage of the cancer and the overall health of the patient:

Surgery

  • Why It's Performed: Surgery is the only potential cure for pancreatic cancer but is only possible if the cancer is localized and hasn’t spread to other organs.
  • Goal: The goal of surgery is to remove as much of the tumor as possible and potentially provide a cure if the cancer is found at an early stage.

Chemotherapy

  • Why It's Performed: Chemotherapy uses drugs to kill cancer cells or stop them from growing. It can be used before surgery to shrink the tumor, after surgery to kill remaining cancer cells.
  • Goal: To reduce tumor size, improve symptoms, or prolong survival. Chemotherapy may also be combined with other treatments for better results.

Radiation Therapy

  • Why It's Performed: Radiation uses high-energy rays to kill cancer cells or shrink tumors. It can be used before surgery to shrink the tumor, after surgery to kill any remaining cancer cells, or as a palliative treatment to reduce symptoms like pain.
  • Goal: To control tumor growth, relieve symptoms (such as pain or blockage), and help improve quality of life.

Targeted Therapy

  • Why It's Performed: Targeted therapy involves drugs that specifically target cancer cells without affecting normal cells. These drugs target specific molecules involved in cancer cell growth and survival.
  • Goal: To stimulate the immune system to attack and destroy cancer cells.

Palliative Care

  • Why It's Performed: In cases where the cancer is in an advanced stage and cannot be cured, palliative care focuses on relieving symptoms, improving comfort, and enhancing the quality of life.
  • Goal: The goal is to improving quality of life and symptom management in advanced stages.
